Telecommunications and Networks

Technologies related to communication systems are present everywhere today: there are more mobile phones than inhabitants, the Internet has become an essential global network and computer networks via Wifi are multiplying.

The Telecommunications License provides training that is part of the dynamics of the professions associated with the world of digital communications (administration of information systems and networks, operation of telecommunications systems or web technologies) and affect many sectors of activities (telecommunications operators, corporate networks, IT service companies, etc.). Thus, students learn how communications are carried out in networks, how information is exchanged between computers and what physical principles are involved. A significant part of their program is devoted to the study of the structure and the organization of fixed or mobile networks

Definition of Telecommunications

Telecommunications is defined as the transmission of information over a distance using electronic, computer, wired, optical or electromagnetic technologies. This term has a broader meaning than its official equivalent “electronic communication”. They are thus distinguished from the post office, which transmits information or objects in physical form.
Telecommunications, the beginnings of which date, among other things, from smoke signals and the optical telegraph, have concerned since the beginning of the 20th century with the use of electrical and then electronic equipment associated with analog or digital networks such as fixed and mobile telephones, radio, television or computers connected to the Internet. They are an important part of the economy and are regulated globally.
Robert Hooke invented the first telephone in history in 1667: the string telephone. Subsequently, in the 19th century, inventors such as Antonio Meucci, Alexander Graham Bell or Guglielmo Marconi developed communication devices such as the telegraph, the telephone or the radio. These revolutionized traditional means such as flags or the Chappe optical telegraph.
The installation under the oceans of submarine cables connected to terrestrial networks allows the transit of data over very long distances from one continent to another.
